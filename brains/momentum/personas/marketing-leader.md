# Marketing Leader

## Company
Momentum

## Role/Title
Marketing Leader, VP/Director Marketing, CMO. Owns demand gen, messaging, and pipeline contribution; aligns with sales and product on buyer insights.

## Responsibilities
- Understand what messaging and campaigns resonate; which competitors show up and how buyers respond
- Find case study candidates and proof from happy customers (quotes, recordings)
- Feed product and sales with market and competitive insights from the field

## Key Problems & Pain Points
- Market and competitive insights trapped in calls; postmortems too late for campaign or messaging decisions
- No systematic way to find “happiest customers” for case studies and references
- Disconnect between campaign messaging and what actually lands in conversations
- Slow or manual feedback loop from sales to marketing

## Goals & Objectives
- Market research on autopilot: which competitors pop up, messaging effectiveness
- Automated alerts in Slack with quotes and recordings from happiest customers for case study candidate list
- Alerts when buyers react to campaign messaging so marketing can optimize same day
- Democratized GTM data so entire org has insights from go-to-market motion

## How Momentum Solves Their Problems
- **Deep Research / Ask Momentum** — Insights from customer engagements: competitors, messaging effectiveness; on-demand answers (e.g. how a pitch landed)
- **Signal routing** — Marketing alerted when buyers react to campaign messaging; competitor mentions and objection trends routed to Marketing in Slack/Teams
- **Case study candidates** — Automated alerts with quotes and recordings from happy customers to build case study list
- **AI CRO / Data** — “Democratize insights to the whole organization”; “ton of data hidden in customer engagements—Momentum pulls it out”

## Buying Process
May evaluate with RevOps and sales; CMO or demand gen lead. Cares about messaging intelligence, case study pipeline, and alignment with sales. Proof: “response time from 13 days to 2” (Sound Agriculture, VP Marketing); market research and case study use cases on solutions page.

## Communication Preferences
“Market research on autopilot,” “find case study candidates,” “on-demand insights from a call or account,” “messaging effectiveness.” Proof from VP Marketing (Sound Agriculture).
