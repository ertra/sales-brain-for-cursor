## Competitive Battlecard

### When We Win
- Buyer wants one brand for multiple form factors (spinner + slider + ring or pendant)
- Buyer values clear series and co-brands (collectible “universe”)
- Buyer wants wearable (ring/pendant) with same brand as desk/pocket
- Buyer responds to “Stay Cool & Be Different” and in-house manufacturing story
- Buyer wants an entry-level option from a premium brand (not only $100+)

### When They Win
- Buyer is loyal to a specific niche brand (e.g. spinner-only community)
- Buyer wants a material or design only that competitor offers (e.g. specific titanium run)
- Buyer found competitor first and isn’t comparing broadly

### Key Differentiators (Our Advantages)
1. **Full lineup under one brand**: Spinners, sliders, rings, pendants, EDC/DIY—one story, one quality bar.
2. **Wearable depth**: Mechanic rings and pendant line for discreet use; many premium brands don’t offer equivalent.
3. **Series and co-brands**: 2099, Spy Wars, EDC House, PELICAN, DZ, UrbanEDC, OneBean—clear “universe” to collect.
4. **In-house + story**: “Lautie factory,” Yangman & Niko; quality and identity in one narrative.
5. **Entry to flagship**: From ~$5 to $600 so “premium” isn’t only high-ticket.

### Handling “I’m looking at [Other Brand] too”
1. Acknowledge their quality or community if you know it.
2. Ask what they’re looking for: “Just spinners, or also something for meetings/travel?” (Rings/pendants = us.)
3. Highlight one brand for everything: “We do spinners, sliders, rings, and pendants so you can stay in one ecosystem if you want to expand later.”
4. Mention series and co-brands if they’re into collecting: “We’ve got a clear series lineup and co-brands like PELICAN and DZ so there’s a lot to collect under one roof.”
