# Target Companies

## Overview
Attio sells to the next generation of companies and their GTM teams — from individuals and small teams to scale-ups and enterprises. Positioning: "The CRM behind thousands of companies." Target segments include SaaS startups, product-led and sales-led teams, scale-ups, SMBs, and investors (deal flow). Customers use Attio for a single source of truth, workflow automation, AI-enriched relationships, product-to-revenue signals, and deal flow at scale.

**Referenced customers (from site/customers page):** Granola (SaaS, $63M Series B), Modal (AI, $23M Series A), Flatfile (Data Migration, $94.8M Series B), Legora (AI, $115M Series B), Railway (SaaS, $20M Series A), Seedcamp (Venture Capital), USV (Venture Capital), Bravado. Also cited elsewhere: Hightouch, Snackpass.

**Case study themes:** 83% faster lead triage (Granola); workflow automation at scale / PLG (Modal); single source of truth for GTM (Flatfile); AI-enriched GTM (Legora); usage data into revenue (Railway); deal engine at scale (Seedcamp); siloed data into source of truth (USV).

## Company Profiles

### Startups / small teams
- **Industry**: SaaS, AI, tech, data/DevTools; any B2B with early GTM
- **Size**: Small teams to ~50 employees; 1–10 GTM users; often pre-Series B or early growth
- **Characteristics**: Need a CRM that fits their model without long implementation; want free or low-cost start (Free plan); care about product data + CRM in one place
- **Why They Buy**: Get started with no credit card; real-time sync and enrichment; flexible data model; scale without ripping out the CRM later
- **Common Challenges**: Legacy CRM too rigid; months of setup; no product/usage context in CRM; tool sprawl

### Scale-ups / mid-market
- **Industry**: B2B SaaS, AI, infrastructure, data/DevTools, professional services
- **Size**: ~50–500+ employees; dedicated SDR/CS/RevOps; Series A–C typical
- **Characteristics**: Scaling PLG, PLS, or sales-led motion; need workflows, sequences, and reporting that keep up; care about one source of truth across product and CRM
- **Why They Buy**: Plus/Pro plans for automations, Ask Attio, reporting, sequences; 83% faster triage (Granola-style); workflow automation at scale (Modal-style); single source of truth (Flatfile-style)
- **Common Challenges**: Fragmented data; manual triage and routing; CRM doesn’t reflect business model; reporting in separate BI tools

### Enterprise
- **Industry**: Cross-industry; strong fit where GTM is data- and workflow-intensive
- **Size**: Large GTM orgs; complex security, compliance, and integration requirements
- **Characteristics**: Need Enterprise plan: unlimited objects/teams, advanced security & admin, SSO, migration service, invoice billing
- **Why They Buy**: Scale (millions of records, sub-50ms); GDPR, CCPA, ISO; one platform for workflows, AI, data, and reporting; reduce CRM + tool sprawl
- **Common Challenges**: Legacy CRM lock-in; siloed data (USV-style “fragmented to unified”); governance and permissions at scale

### Product-led growth / product-led sales
- **Industry**: SaaS, AI, infrastructure, developer tools
- **Size**: Any; often growth-stage with product usage data
- **Characteristics**: Need product + CRM data unified; lead/account scoring from usage; workflows that react to product events
- **Why They Buy**: Combine product and CRM data in real time; workflows for PLG (e.g. pass to CS, expansion); reporting on workspaces, seats, MRR; AI on top of product context
- **Common Challenges**: CRM blind to product usage; manual handoffs from product to sales/CS; no single view of account health

### Investors / deal flow
- **Industry**: Venture capital, PE, investment firms
- **Size**: Small (single fund) to large (many partners and associates)
- **Characteristics**: Track deals, companies, funds, relationships; need flexible objects (e.g. Fund, Deal, Company) and pipeline by stage
- **Why They Buy**: Deal flow management; “perfect deal engine” (Seedcamp); turn siloed data into source of truth (USV); custom data model for investing workflow
- **Common Challenges**: Deals in spreadsheets or legacy tools; no single source of truth; poor visibility across pipeline and relationships
