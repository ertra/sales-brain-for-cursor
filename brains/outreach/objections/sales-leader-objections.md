# Sales Leader - Objection Handling

## Overview
Common objections from sales leaders and responses focused on forecast confidence, productivity lift, and predictable execution.

## Objection Plays

### 1. Risk/Change: "I don't want to disrupt my team mid-quarter"
- **Why They Say This**: Fear of productivity dip during rollout.
- **Acknowledge**: "Protecting in-quarter execution is critical."
- **Respond**: "We can phase by one motion and one manager cohort to avoid broad disruption."
- **Proof Point**: Outreach supports package-based rollout by workflow.
- **Follow-Up Question**: "Which workflow can we improve with lowest in-quarter risk first?"

### 2. Price/Budget: "This seems expensive right now"
- **Why They Say This**: Budget is tied to near-term commitments.
- **Acknowledge**: "Understood, especially with quarterly pressure."
- **Respond**: "Let's tie cost to one measurable outcome like forecast variance reduction or rep productivity gain."
- **Proof Point**: Customer listings include +15% reps hitting quota and productivity claims.
- **Follow-Up Question**: "What KPI outcome would make this investment acceptable?"

### 3. Timing: "Let's revisit after this quarter"
- **Why They Say This**: Competing priorities and limited management bandwidth.
- **Acknowledge**: "That timing concern is valid."
- **Respond**: "A small pilot now can reduce risk and give you evidence before next planning cycle."
- **Proof Point**: Modular packages and customer references with measurable outcomes.
- **Follow-Up Question**: "Would a scoped 60-day pilot help de-risk next quarter decisions?"

## Operator Guidance
### Questions to Ask Early
1. Which revenue KPI is under most executive pressure?
2. What level of rollout risk is acceptable this quarter?
3. Who must sign off on forecast/process changes?
4. What result is needed to justify expansion?

### Disqualification Criteria
- No executive ownership for forecast/productivity outcomes.
- No sponsor willing to run even a limited pilot.

### Talk Tracks
- **15s**: We can improve predictability without disrupting your quarter through phased rollout.
- **30s**: Start with one workflow and one KPI, then expand once outcome is proven.
- **2min**: Leadership objections usually focus on risk and timing. A tightly scoped pilot gives measurable proof while preserving execution continuity, which is often the fastest path to confident adoption.

### Next-Step CTA
- Approve pilot scope: one team, one KPI, one quarter checkpoint.
