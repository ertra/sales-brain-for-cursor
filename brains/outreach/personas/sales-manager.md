# Sales Manager

## Overview
Sales managers are frontline operators responsible for pipeline health, rep coaching, and forecast call quality. They buy when they need faster insight into rep/deal risk and clearer coaching levers.

## Role and Context
- **Role/Title**: Sales Manager, Director of Sales, Frontline Manager.
- **Core Responsibilities**: Team pipeline review, rep coaching, deal inspection, forecast input quality.
- **Typical Company Context**: Growing teams where manager bandwidth is constrained.

## Pains, Drivers, and Priorities
- **Top Pain Points**:
  - Limited time for high-quality coaching at scale.
  - Difficulty spotting pipeline risk early.
  - Inconsistent rep execution and follow-through.
- **Business Priorities**:
  - Improve rep performance and consistency.
  - Increase team pipeline quality and conversion.
  - Build dependable inspection cadence.
- **Buying Triggers**:
  - Team misses due to late risk detection.
  - New manager span/control reduces coaching quality.
  - Need standardized process for one-to-ones and deal reviews.

## Buying Behavior
- **Decision Criteria**: Workflow usability for reps/managers, visibility into risk, measurable performance impact.
- **Stakeholders**: Sales leadership, RevOps, Enablement.
- **Common Risks/Concerns**: Manager adoption burden, rep pushback, noisy alerts without actionability.

## Messaging Guidance
- **What Resonates**: Pipeline health visibility, action-oriented coaching, less admin, faster interventions.
- **What to Avoid**: Abstract platform messaging disconnected from day-to-day manager workflows.
