# Revenue Operations

## Overview
Revenue Operations leaders own process integrity, pipeline governance, and system-level revenue visibility. They buy when current tooling causes leakage, inefficiency, and unreliable forecasting.

## Role and Context
- **Role/Title**: VP/Director of RevOps, GTM Operations leader.
- **Core Responsibilities**: Pipeline governance, process design, reporting, system integration, forecasting support.
- **Typical Company Context**: Scaling or enterprise revenue organizations with multiple GTM systems.

## Pains, Drivers, and Priorities
- **Top Pain Points**:
  - Pipeline leakage and inconsistent stage hygiene.
  - Disjointed workflows across engagement, deal, and forecast processes.
  - High manual effort to reconcile data and prepare forecast views.
- **Business Priorities**:
  - Improve data/process consistency across teams.
  - Reduce operational complexity from tool sprawl.
  - Increase forecast confidence and reporting reliability.
- **Buying Triggers**:
  - Executive demand for more reliable forecasting.
  - Ongoing process breakdowns at pipeline stage transitions.
  - Strategic initiative to consolidate revenue tech stack.

## Buying Behavior
- **Decision Criteria**: Integration readiness, governance controls, workflow configurability, measurable process outcomes.
- **Stakeholders**: Sales leadership, IT/security, Finance, frontline managers.
- **Common Risks/Concerns**: Migration effort, data quality risk, implementation complexity.

## Messaging Guidance
- **What Resonates**: Pipeline integrity, workflow consistency, one platform, governance, predictable operations.
- **What to Avoid**: Vague AI claims without operational control and KPI proof.
