# Customer Success Manager

## Overview
Customer Success Managers own retention and expansion outcomes after handoff from sales. They buy when they need better context continuity, fewer redundant conversations, and earlier risk detection for renewals.

## Role and Context
- **Role/Title**: Customer Success Manager, Account Manager, Renewal Manager.
- **Core Responsibilities**: Renewal health, expansion identification, relationship management, business outcome alignment.
- **Typical Company Context**: B2B recurring-revenue businesses with post-sales growth targets.

## Pains, Drivers, and Priorities
- **Top Pain Points**:
  - Poor context transfer from pre-sales to post-sales.
  - Late visibility into churn risk signals.
  - Repetitive customer interactions that reduce trust and efficiency.
- **Business Priorities**:
  - Improve retention and expansion predictability.
  - Align account conversations to measurable customer outcomes.
  - Reduce friction between sales and CS workflows.
- **Buying Triggers**:
  - Rising churn or renewal slippage.
  - Expansion pipeline underperformance.
  - Need for coordinated account workflows across teams.

## Buying Behavior
- **Decision Criteria**: Account visibility, risk signal quality, workflow coordination with sales, adoption simplicity.
- **Stakeholders**: CS leadership, Sales leadership, RevOps, Account teams.
- **Common Risks/Concerns**: Too sales-centric workflow, insufficient post-sales signal relevance.

## Messaging Guidance
- **What Resonates**: Retention risk visibility, shared account context, outcome-based customer engagement.
- **What to Avoid**: Messaging focused only on net-new pipeline without lifecycle value.
