# Sales Leader - Objection Handling

## Overview
Common objections from Sales Leaders (VP Sales, BDR Leader, Director of Business Development) and how to handle them with Apollo-specific proof and follow-ups.

## Objections

### 1. Headcount / Productivity: "We need more SDRs, not a new tool"

**Why They Say This**: Assumes pipeline is a headcount problem; hasn’t seen productivity gains from one platform.

**Acknowledge**: "Hiring is one lever. Many leaders find they can get more from the same team first."

**Respond**: "Apollo is built to multiply what each rep can do. GTM Ops gets as many ICP meetings with one SDR as they used to with three. Cyera booked 75% more meetings while cutting manual work in half. So you can hit the same or higher pipeline with your current team, or prove productivity before adding headcount. One platform—data, sequences, dialer—means less ramp and less tool fatigue."

**Proof Point**:
- GTM Ops: 4x meetings per SDR (one SDR = three previously)
- Cyera: 75% more meetings, 50% less manual work
- Predictable Revenue: speed to booking cut in half

**Follow-Up Question**: "If you could get 75% more meetings from your current team without adding heads, would that change the plan?"

### 2. Price/Budget: "We don't have the budget for another tool"

**Why They Say This**: Thinks Apollo is additive; doesn’t yet see replacement/consolidation.

**Acknowledge**: "Nobody wants to add cost. Apollo usually replaces cost."

**Respond**: "We’re not asking you to add—we’re asking you to replace. Predictable Revenue cut cost 50% by moving from three tools to Apollo. Census cut tech stack cost 64%. If you’re on ZoomInfo and Outreach today, one Apollo contract is typically half of that combined spend, with data and sequences and dialer in one. Plus there’s a free tier and trial so you can prove it before switching."

**Proof Point**:
- 50% cost savings (Predictable Revenue); 64% lower tech stack (Census)
- Free Starter; trial; cancel anytime (pricing FAQ)

**Follow-Up Question**: "What’s your total spend right now on data and outreach? We can show the comparison to one Apollo plan."

### 3. Timing: "We're in the middle of quarter / not ready to switch"

**Why They Say This**: Focus on number; fear of disruption; or deferring decision.

**Acknowledge**: "Quarter focus is real. No one wants to disrupt the team mid-cycle."

**Respond**: "You don’t have to switch overnight. Start with a pilot team or free tier—no budget approval needed—and run it alongside what you have. Most teams see 75% more meetings and 50% less manual work within a few weeks. When your ZoomInfo or Outreach renewal comes up, that’s the natural moment to consolidate and lock in savings. Waiting usually means another year of double spend."

**Proof Point**:
- Cyera, GTM Ops, Predictable Revenue outcomes in short timeframes
- Trial and free plan = low risk

**Follow-Up Question**: "When does your current outreach or data contract renew? We can time a pilot to that."

## Objection Prevention

### Questions to Ask Early
1. "How much are you spending on data and outreach tools combined, and when do those renew?"
2. "How many meetings do you need per rep per month to hit pipeline, and are you there today?"
3. "Who’s involved in renewing or changing sales tech—just you, or RevOps, finance?"
4. "Are you open to consolidating tools if it cut cost and increased output?"

### Red Flags to Watch For
- "We just signed a 2-year with ZoomInfo" (timing; note renewal for next time)
- "Our reps love Outreach" (don’t attack; position Apollo as data + sequences in one, same workflow, lower cost)
- "We need a formal RFP" (offer ROI doc, security, and customer references)
