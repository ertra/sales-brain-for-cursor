# Target Companies

## Overview
Apollo sells to B2B organizations that run sales and marketing motions—from rapidly growing startups to large enterprises. Over 500,000 companies use Apollo; 40,000+ are paying customers, and 1M+ sales professionals use the platform. Target segments include companies that want to build pipeline (outbound/inbound), enrich and cleanse data, consolidate sales tech, and close deals with less manual work. Apollo explicitly serves SMB through Fortune 500 (e.g. Ernst & Young, Oracle, Lyft); enterprise often uses Custom plans for integrations, security, and governance.

**Referenced customers (from site/reviews):** Predictable Revenue, Neurofit, GTM Ops, Cyera, Customer.io, Census, Built In, Leadium, FLO EV, Bitrise, Plative, TopOut Group, Tessell, Noble, Emerge Market.

## Company Profiles

### Rapidly growing startups / SMB
- **Industry**: Any B2B; strong in SaaS, tech, agencies, professional services
- **Size**: Small teams to ~100 employees; often 1–20 sales/marketing users
- **Characteristics**: Need to scale pipeline without proportional headcount; limited budget for multiple tools; want free or low-cost start (Starter plan, trials)
- **Why They Buy**: Single platform for data + outreach + meetings; AI to do more with fewer reps; replace ZoomInfo + Outreach + dialer with one vendor; improve reply rates and meeting volume
- **Common Challenges**: Manual prospecting, tool sprawl, unverified data, slow follow-up on inbound

### Mid-market / scale-up
- **Industry**: B2B SaaS, fintech, healthcare, manufacturing, services
- **Size**: ~100–2,000 employees; dedicated BDR/SDR teams, RevOps, sometimes marketing ops
- **Characteristics**: Scaling outbound and inbound; care about data quality, routing, and CRM hygiene; open to consolidating data provider + engagement + dialer
- **Why They Buy**: 4x SDR efficiency, 50%+ cost savings vs. multiple tools; better data quality and enrichment; deal execution (calls, pipeline, coaching) in one place
- **Common Challenges**: Inefficient research, low connect rates, cold leads going stale, fragmented pipeline visibility

### Enterprise / Fortune 500
- **Industry**: Cross-industry; named examples include professional services (E&Y), tech (Oracle, Lyft)
- **Size**: Large sales and GTM organizations; complex security and integration needs
- **Characteristics**: Need Custom plans, advanced integrations, API, governance, and compliance (SOC 2, GDPR, etc.)
- **Why They Buy**: One platform for data, outbound, inbound, enrichment, and deal execution; enterprise-grade security and privacy; reduce vendor count and cost
- **Common Challenges**: Tool consolidation, data accuracy at scale, global compliance, rep productivity and coaching

### Sales-led / outbound-first
- **Industry**: Any B2B where outbound is a primary motion
- **Size**: Any; from solo founders to large SDR/BDR teams
- **Characteristics**: Heavy outbound; care about meetings booked, reply/open rates, and speed to meeting
- **Why They Buy**: Book more meetings with less grind; AI lists and campaigns; built-in deliverability; parallel dialer and Chrome extension
- **Common Challenges**: Manual list-building, low response rates, deliverability issues, too many tools

### RevOps / data-driven GTM
- **Industry**: SaaS, tech, high-velocity sales
- **Size**: Mid-market and enterprise with dedicated RevOps
- **Characteristics**: Own data quality, routing, enrichment, CRM sync, and reporting
- **Why They Buy**: Enrichment and waterfall; CRM and API; 50%+ data quality gains; replace ZoomInfo/Clearbit; one data source for routing and personalization
- **Common Challenges**: Stale data, multiple enrichment vendors, routing accuracy, stack cost
