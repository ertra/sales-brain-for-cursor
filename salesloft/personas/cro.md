# Persona: Chief Revenue Officer (CRO)

## Role Overview
The CRO is the executive responsible for all revenue-generating activities across the organization. They oversee sales, marketing, customer success, and revenue operations to drive predictable, sustainable growth. They report to the CEO and board on revenue performance.

## Titles
- Chief Revenue Officer (CRO)
- Chief Commercial Officer (CCO)
- Chief Sales Officer (CSO)
- President of Sales
- EVP of Revenue
- EVP of Global Sales

## Reports To
- CEO
- Board of Directors

## Key Responsibilities
- Drive overall revenue growth
- Align sales, marketing, and CS teams
- Own revenue forecast and reporting to board
- Set go-to-market strategy
- Build and scale revenue organization
- Optimize revenue efficiency
- Manage P&L for revenue functions
- Drive market expansion

## Pain Points

### 1. Unpredictable Revenue
- Can't confidently forecast to board
- Deals slip unexpectedly
- Pipeline coverage doesn't translate to closed revenue
- Economic uncertainty increases risk

### 2. GTM Misalignment
- Sales, marketing, CS working in silos
- Different tools, different data
- Finger-pointing when targets missed
- No single source of truth

### 3. Revenue Efficiency Pressure
- "Do more with less" mandate
- Need to prove tech stack ROI
- Board scrutiny on spend
- Scaling without proportional headcount

### 4. Talent and Performance
- Top performers leaving
- Wide variance in rep performance
- Long ramp times for new hires
- Coaching isn't scalable

## Buying Triggers
- New CRO joining organization (first 90 days)

- IPO preparation requiring predictability
- Missed revenue targets
- GTM reorganization
- M&A integration
